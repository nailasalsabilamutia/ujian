Spring

The characteristics of spring include the blooming of plants and flowers, so this season is also known as the “flower season”. The air temperature begins to warm up after winter, the days become longer with a longer duration of day than night.


Summer

Summer is the season that follows spring, usually in subtropical climates, characterized by warmer temperatures between 26°-31°C.

winter

Winter is the coldest season that occurs in subtropical and temperate climates, usually lasting about three months. Characteristics of winter include very low temperatures, often below 0 degrees Celsius.


Autumn

Fall is the transitional season from summer to winter that occurs in temperate and subtropical regions.

facts about the four seasons

The four seasons-spring, summer, fall and winter-are part of an annual cycle that occurs due to the Earth's position relative to the Sun. Autumn brings leaf color changes and a drop in temperature, while winter is the coldest season with snow and longer nights.

Spring and summer support growth and activity, while fall and winter are a time of preparation and adaptation to colder weather.
